    Interest in IT

    When did your interest in IT start?

    I first became interested in IT at around the age of 5-6 through a program called SketchUp, a simple 3D modelling application. Interacting with the virtual objects onscreen was like magic to me, and I would spend hours experimenting with different designs and combinations every day.

    What is your interest in IT?

    I’m interested in IT for two reasons, the first of which is the potential it holds for my own personal expression. I’m an artist at heart, and I think that having a solid grasp of the fundamentals of IT and programming will allow me to see my future ambitions through to completion.

    The second reason is the career security that an IT qualification provides; the world is becoming more influenced by IT every day. So, having a pathway into that industry through a degree is incredibly advantageous.

    Why RMIT?

    Two years ago, I would never have pictured myself at University. I was working as a video editor with a small production company, and pretty was satisfied. It wasn’t long after my company started receiving work from various Australian universities, advertisements mainly, and I was tasked with editing them. Long story short, I edited so many University ads, analysed so many student testimonials, and listened to so much sales talk that I think I ended up selling myself on pursuing a degree, bringing me here.

    What do you expect to learn?

    I hope to learn the fundamentals of many relevant technologies, become familiar with using various programming languages and 3rd party applications, learn basic web development, and get an appreciation for working in a team environment similar to that of in the industry. Writing is a good indication of understanding, but understanding is not an indication of experience, so I hope to acquire a hands-on level of practical knowledge to go with it.
    Hopefully, I will get to engage with the technology as much as I write about it.
